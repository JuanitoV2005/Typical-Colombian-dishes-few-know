# Casabe-de-Yuca-Brava

Synopsis 

<p align="justify">
Casabe is a typical dish in the north of south america, that consists of a tortilla made with yuca flour. Its origin goes back to the pre-Columbian age, where the native americans, arahuacos and caribbean  produced it due yuca was so common in the areas where they lived; in Colombia it is registered until 1000 a.c as indigenous of the community of  Panágua, which inhabited the current region of  Ciénega de Oro in Cordoba, produced this food. In the book “Ciénaga de Oro ayer y hoy” is  mentioned  its performance in the colonial age as an important piece in the Spanish conquest in America, since it was the displacement of wheat flour and by its easy conservation. Its preparation consists in grated yuca for getting grated yuca pulp, which is dry and squeezed for then work it with mortar and pestle, after that it is put in a sifter for getting a fine flour with which to mold a tortilla, finally it is charred. Today it is consumed in different places of Colombia and in 2024 UNESCO officially recognized the practices associated with its preparation and consumption as Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.
