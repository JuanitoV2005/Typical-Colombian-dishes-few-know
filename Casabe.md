# **Cassava bread**

## *Synopsis*

<p align="justify">
Cassava bread is a typical dish in the north of south america, that consists of a tortilla made with yuca flour. Its origin goes back to the pre-Columbian age, where the native americans, arahuacos and caribbean  produced it due yuca was so common in the areas where they lived; in Colombia it is registered until 1000 a.c as indigenous of the community of  Panágua, which inhabited the current region of  Ciénega de Oro in Cordoba, produced this food. In the book “Ciénaga de Oro ayer y hoy” is  mentioned  its performance in the colonial age as an important piece in the Spanish conquest in America, since it was the displacement of wheat flour and by its easy conservation. Its preparation consists in grated yuca for getting grated yuca pulp, which is dry and squeezed for then work it with mortar and pestle, after that it is put in a sifter for getting a fine flour with which to mold a tortilla, finally it is charred. Today it is consumed in different places of Colombia and in 2024 UNESCO officially recognized the practices associated with its preparation and consumption as Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.
</p>

## *Review*

<p align="justify">
Cassava bread is an ancestral food from the pre-Columbian cultures, it was developed by different indigenous cultures around the Caribbean sea. The knowledge of the recipe has come to nowadays society through orally between generations. Even the bread counts with multiple origins, the main process of the recipe is similar for the cultures that had cooked. For that reason and the developed process of desintoxicate cassava, UNESCO decided to recognise it as an Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity in 2024. This bread has history in countries such as Cuba, the Dominican Republic, Haiti, Honduras, Venezuela and Colombia, that justify the inclusion of the dish in our work.
</p>

<p align="justify">
This bread has two principal characteristics, the first one is that cassava bread is an unleavened bread, which means that it is made without yeast. The second one is that preparation of cassava bread requires a detoxification process because its main ingredient is cassava. Cassava can be poisonous to humans if it is not prepared correctly, since it may contain compounds that can produce hydrogen cyanide. This makes cassava bread fascinating because it shows the creativity and knowledge of Indigenous communities in food preparation.
</p>

<p align="justify">
The process of making cassava bread consists of eight steps. The first three steps focus on preparing the cassava: washing, peeling, cutting, and removing the fibrous core. The fourth and fifth steps are the most important because the cassava is grated and pressed to release the toxic liquid. Finally, the remaining steps focus on making the bread or crackers. This part begins with drying the cassava mass left from the previous process and ends with cooking it.
</p>
