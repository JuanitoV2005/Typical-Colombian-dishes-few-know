# **Asado llanero**

## *Synopsis*

<p align="justify">
The orinoquia region is also known as “llanos orientales”. This region is a huge extension of open savannahs that include a part of Colombia and Venezuela. It is a territory with a lot of rivers and different kinds of animals, such as the cachama, palomera y payana. The climate is a relationship between drought and rain, and the native vegetation includes the yopo tree which is useful green and dry to produce fire for cooking. 
</p>

<p align="justify">  
Historically, the region has been defined by three related elements, cattle ranching, the horse and fire. These are not simple economic elements, in fact the elements represent the base of the llanero cultural identity. The Orinoco gastronomy reflects this reality: dishes like mamona, chiguiro, tungos, hallacas llaneras and the casabe are not aleatory or isolated recipes. Rather the expressions showing a way of life built the communitarian work and the cattle ranching.
</p>
